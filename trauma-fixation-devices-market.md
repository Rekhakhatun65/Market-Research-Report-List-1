<p><ul><li>Internal Fixation Devices</li><li>External Fixation Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Trauma fixation devices are essential surgical tools used to stabilize fractures and enhance healing. Internal fixation devices include plates, screws, and nails that are surgically implanted within the body to support bone alignment during recovery. In contrast, external fixation devices are applied externally, utilizing pins and rods to stabilize fractures from outside the body. Both device types serve critical roles in trauma management, offering different approaches depending on fracture severity, location, and patient needs. Each type aids in promoting effective recovery and minimizing complications.</p></p>
