<p><ul><li>Natural Trans Fatty Acids</li><li>Synthetic Trans Fatty Acids</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The trans fatty acids market comprises two main types: natural and synthetic trans fatty acids. Natural trans fatty acids are formed in the stomachs of ruminant animals and are found in small amounts in certain dairy and meat products. In contrast, synthetic trans fatty acids are produced during hydrogenation, a process that solidifies liquid oils, primarily used in processed foods, margarine, and baked goods. The demand for natural trans fats is growing due to health-conscious consumers, while regulations increasingly target synthetic variants.</p></p>
