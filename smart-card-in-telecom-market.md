<p><p>Smart cards are physical devices equipped with embedded microprocessors that securely store and process data. In the telecom market, there are primarily two types: contact cards and contactless cards. Contact cards require physical insertion into a reader, facilitating secure communication for transactions or identification. Conversely, contactless cards enable quicker interactions through radio frequency identification (RFID) technology, allowing users to simply tap the card near a reader. Both types play critical roles in enhancing security and convenience for telecommunications services.</p></p>
