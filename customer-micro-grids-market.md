<p><ul><li>Grid-Tied Micro Grids</li><li>Independent Micro Grids</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The customer microgrid market is categorized into two main types: grid-tied microgrids and independent microgrids. Grid-tied microgrids are integrated with the larger utility grid, allowing for bi-directional energy flow and enhanced reliability. They can leverage grid resources and provide services like demand response. Independent microgrids operate autonomously and can function without a connection to the main grid, often powered by renewable energy sources. This allows for increased energy resilience, particularly in remote or disaster-prone areas.</p></p>
