<p><p>The semi-solid battery market features two primary types based on the electrolyte used: organic and inorganic. Organic electrolyte semi-solid batteries utilize carbon-based compounds, offering advantages like higher energy density and improved safety profiles. In contrast, inorganic electrolyte semi-solid batteries employ metal salts or oxides, providing enhanced thermal stability and conductivity. Both types aim to bridge the performance gap between traditional batteries and solid-state alternatives, catering to diverse applications in electric vehicles, consumer electronics, and renewable energy storage.</p></p>
