<p><p>The medical-grade stainless steel tube market primarily comprises 304 and 316 grades, along with other specialized types. Grade 304 is favored for its excellent corrosion resistance and formability, commonly used in non-critical applications. Grade 316, containing molybdenum, offers enhanced resistance to chloride corrosion, making it ideal for surgical instruments and implants. Other types may include higher alloyed or specially treated variations, tailored for specific medical uses, ensuring biocompatibility and durability in demanding environments.</p></p>
