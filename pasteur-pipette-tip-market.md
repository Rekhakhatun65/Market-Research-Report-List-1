<p><p>The Pasteur pipette tip market is divided into two primary types: non-filtered and filtered pipette tips. Non-filtered pipette tips are designed for general liquid transfer and provide a straightforward method for moving liquids without additional filtration. On the other hand, filtered pipette tips incorporate a barrier, typically made from hydrophobic materials, to prevent contamination of samples and avoid the aspiration of aerosols into the pipette. Each type serves specific needs in laboratories, influencing their applications based on contamination risk and sample integrity.</p></p>
